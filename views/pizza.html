About Pizza

Pizza is a savory dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flat base of leavened wheat-based dough topped with tomatoes, cheese, and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, vegetables, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.

Types of Pizza

  • Margherita: A classic pizza topped with tomatoes, mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil.
  • Pepperoni: A popular pizza topped with pepperoni slices and mozzarella cheese.
  • Veggie: A pizza loaded with a variety of vegetables like bell peppers, onions, mushrooms, and olives.
  • BBQ Chicken: A pizza topped with barbecue sauce, chicken, red onions, and cilantro.
  • Hawaiian: A controversial pizza topped with ham and pineapple.

Pizza Making Process

The process of making pizza involves several steps:

  1. Preparing the Dough: Mixing flour, water, yeast, and salt to make the dough and letting it rise.
  2. Shaping the Dough: Stretching and shaping the dough into a round base.
  3. Adding Toppings: Spreading tomato sauce, adding cheese, and placing other toppings.
  4. Baking: Baking the pizza in an oven until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
